Retirement Is a Financial Transition, Not a Finish Line

The shift from accumulating wealth to drawing income is one of the most complex financial transitions you’ll face. Getting it right requires coordinating Social Security timing, tax bracket management, withdrawal sequencing, Medicare planning, and portfolio strategy—all at once.

A Plan Built for Your Retirement, Not a Template

We build a retirement plan specific to your circumstances. A year-by-year projection that accounts for your income sources, tax situation, spending needs, Social Security timing, and healthcare costs.

We stress-test the plan against market downturns, inflation, longevity, and unexpected expenses. Then we revisit it regularly because assumptions change and your plan should change with them.

Key Strategies We Coordinate

Each of these decisions affects the others. We model them together, not in isolation.

Social Security Optimization

The difference between the best and worst claiming strategy can be six figures over a lifetime. We model every scenario for your household—spousal benefits, survivor benefits, early vs. delayed, and coordination with other income sources.

Potential impact: $100,000+

Roth Conversion Planning

Converting traditional IRA assets to Roth in low-income years can save significant taxes over a multi-decade retirement. We identify the right amount and timing each year—balancing tax brackets, Medicare thresholds, and future RMD projections.

Potential impact: 0–30% of total wealth

Tax Bracket Management

Strategic withdrawal sequencing across taxable, tax-deferred, and Roth accounts to keep you in the lowest possible tax bracket each year. Which account to draw from, when, and how much—optimized across your full retirement timeline.

Potential impact: 0.50–0.70% annually

Medicare & Healthcare Planning

Income decisions in retirement directly affect Medicare premiums through IRMAA surcharges. A single Roth conversion or capital gain can spike your premiums for two years. We plan around these thresholds before they become a surprise.

Potential impact: Thousands to hundreds of thousands

The Advisor Between You and the Big Mistake

Investors who act on emotion during market stress—selling at lows or chasing performance at highs—systematically underperform their own portfolios.

“Investors who deviated from their target allocation trailed the benchmark by 150 basis points.”

An advisor’s role here isn’t motivational. It’s structural. Having someone to call before making a permanent decision with temporary emotions is worth more than most people realize until they need it.
