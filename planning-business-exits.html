Three Gaps That Determine Your Exit

Every business owner has these gaps. Most don’t know how wide they are until it’s too late to close them.

The Wealth Gap

“Do I have enough outside the business to live the life I want?”

The difference between your total wealth goal after exit and your net worth excluding the business. If your business is 80% of your net worth, this gap is probably wider than you think.

The Value Gap

“What is my business actually worth vs. what it could be worth?”

The difference between your business value today and its potential value if you addressed the risks and built the intangible capital that buyers pay premiums for. This is where CFA-level valuation analysis matters.

The Profit Gap

“Am I leaving money on the table every year I still own this?”

The difference between what your business earns now and what it could earn operating at best-in-class levels. Closing this gap increases both your income today and your exit price tomorrow.

What We Coordinate for Business Owners

The financial decisions start years before the exit event. We help define the end goal and reverse-engineer the path.

Exit & Succession Planning

Sell to a third party, transfer to family, management buyout, or close down. The path matters as much as the price. We define the end goal and build the timeline to get there.

Business Valuation Analysis

As a CFA charterholder, Todd brings institutional-grade valuation expertise. We assess your business objectively—what it’s worth today, what drives the number, and what moves it higher before you sell.

Tax-Efficient Exit Structuring

The tax consequences of a business sale can be enormous. Asset sale vs. stock sale, installment strategies, Qualified Small Business Stock exclusions, charitable planning—we model every scenario before closing day.

Tax-Efficient Compensation

Salary vs. distributions. Retirement plan contributions. Health insurance structuring. We evaluate the full picture to minimize what goes to the IRS while you still own the business.

Retirement Plan Design

SEP IRA, Solo 401(k), defined benefit plan, or cash balance plan? The right structure depends on income, employees, and deferral goals. We model the options and coordinate with your CPA.

Key Person & Buy-Sell Planning

Protecting the business and your family from the financial impact of losing a key person. Structuring buy-sell agreements funded by insurance so the business transitions cleanly if the unexpected happens.

The Credentials That Back This Up

Most advisors offer exit planning as a side conversation. We hold the two designations that make it a core competency.

Designation

CEPA — Certified Exit Planning Advisor

The most widely recognized exit planning credential in the world. CEPAs are trained to align business, personal, and financial goals into one integrated transition strategy—not just find a buyer, but build a plan that works for the owner, the family, and the business.

Designation

CFA — Chartered Financial Analyst

The global gold standard for investment analysis and valuation. Fewer than 2% of advisors hold the CFA charter. It takes 1,000+ hours of study and covers financial analysis, portfolio management, and—critically for business owners—equity valuation methodology at an institutional level.
